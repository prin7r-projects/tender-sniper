## Channel-specific playbooks

### Channel 1 — NCMA / GovCon Slack
- **Posture**: Be a useful contributor first. Share the bid-readiness scoring model as a free PDF in `#bd-tools-discussion`. Don't link the website in the same message.
- **Cadence**: 1 post/week, 2 helpful replies/day, 0 product-pitches.
- **Conversion path**: Bio link → site → checkout. The peer recommendation is the funnel.

### Channel 2 — Newsletters (FedScoop, Federal News Network, FCW)
- **Earned**: Pitch a 1,200-word op-ed: *"Why your saved search returns 70% noise — a capture manager's diagnostic."* Land this in FedScoop in week 6-8.
- **Sponsored**: A 4-week run in the FedScoop daily newsletter, targeting capture managers. Test creative: the *live alert ticker* as a static screenshot with a `Read the score model →` CTA.

### Channel 3 — EU CEPS / LinkedIn
- **Posture**: Dieter's audience is more formal than US GovCon. Lead with a CPV-cross-walk whitepaper, not a Slack-style chat. LinkedIn post + downloadable PDF.
- **Speakership**: pitch a CEPS Frankfurt working-group session: *"How CPV semantics fail mid-market consulting firms."*

### Channel 5 — Direct outbound
- **List**: Sales Navigator filter for `Capture Manager` title + NAICS-541512/541611 firms with 50-1,000 employees + last-30-day-active LinkedIn.
- **Cadence**: 30 contacts/wk, three-step sequence: LinkedIn DM (week 1) → personalized email (week 1) → second email with the readiness-score PDF (week 2).
- **Personalization**: name a specific lot from SAM.gov in their NAICS that we'd score above 0.85 — *"This SBA lot dropped Tuesday; Brassmark scored it 0.91 against your firm's NAICS profile. Worth your morning?"*

### Channel 6 — SEO
- Three pillar pages: "SAM.gov saved search false positives," "GovWin alternative for [SDVOSB / 8(a) / WOSB / HUBZone]," "EU TED filter by CPV."
- 6 supporting articles per pillar over 90 days.
- Ranking goal: Top-3 organic for the named comparison queries by month-6.

### Channel 7 — SDVOSB / VOSB / 8(a) / WOSB / HUBZone associations
- **Co-marketing**: 15% discount on Single-region for association members; the association lists Brassmark in their member-benefits page; the association gets a free Federal-Plus seat.

### Channel 10 — Earned PR
- The op-ed pitch: *"The 11.4% problem — why your saved searches' precision is statistically indefensible, and what a defensible bid/no-bid score looks like."*
- Place in FedScoop or FCW. The earned mention of the readiness-score model is what the audience remembers.

## Channel discipline rules

1. **Never** post a Brassmark link in a Slack channel within 30 days of joining.
2. **Always** lead with the *score* in messaging, not the *coverage*. Coverage is an undifferentiated claim across competitors.
3. **No marketing email cadence above 2/month** to a non-customer prospect. The audience will burn the sender domain otherwise.
4. **Never** discount below 15% off list. Discounting devalues the brand in this market.
5. **Never** write copy that hints at lobbying, capitol-imagery, or "make government easier." We are a triage tool for professionals.

## Channel measurement

Per `09-go-to-market.md`, we will track:
- CAC per channel (90-day rolling average).
- LTV:CAC by channel (target: 3:1 minimum at month-12).
- Channel attribution: first-touch + last-touch (not single-touch).
- Channel saturation: when CAC per channel rises 30% above its phase-1 baseline, we ratchet investment back.
